History about Jakarta

Capital City of Jakarta - is the capital city of Indonesia. Jakarta is the only city in Indonesia which has provincial level status. Jakarta is located in the northwestern part of Java Island. Previously known as Sunda Kelapa (before 1527), Jayakarta (1527-1619), Batavia / Batauia, or Jaccatra (1619-1942), and Djakarta (1942-1972).

The name Jakarta has been used since the Japanese occupation in 1942, to refer to the area of the former Gemeente Batavia which was inaugurated by the Dutch East Indies government in 1905. This name is considered as an abbreviation of the word Jayakarta, given by the Demak and Cirebon people under the leadership of Fatahillah (Faletehan) after attacking and occupying the port of Sunda Kelapa on June 22, 1527. This name is usually translated as "city of victory" or "city of glory", but the true meaning is "victory achieved by an act or effort"
